Smt. Droupadi Murmu is the 15th and current President of India, having taken office on July 25, 2022. She is the first person from a tribal community, the youngest, and the first born after independence to hold the position. As the head of state and supreme commander of the armed forces, she serves a largely ceremonial role, with executive powers exercised by the Prime Minister.

Key Details About the President of India:
Current President: Smt. Droupadi Murmu, a former teacher and Governor of Jharkhand.
Term & Election: The President is elected for a five-year term by an electoral college consisting of elected members of both houses of Parliament (MPs) and Legislative Assemblies of the states and union territories.
Constitutional Position: The President is the titular head of the state, with powers to appoint the Prime Minister, Chief Justice, and governors, but acts on the advice of the Council of Ministers.
Powers: The President can declare national, state, or financial emergencies, and has the power to grant pardons.
Eligibility: Must be an Indian citizen, at least 35 years old, and qualified to be a member of the Lok Sabha.
Residence: The President resides in the Rashtrapati Bhavan in New Delhi.
